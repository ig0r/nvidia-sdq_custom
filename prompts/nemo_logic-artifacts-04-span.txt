Extract semantic artifacts from pavement engineering text.
An artifact is a source-grounded text span that expresses a meaningful rule, condition, process, method, value, concept, observation, recommendation, decision, explanation, issue, risk, or supporting evidence.
Use exact source text for extraction_text. Do not paraphrase extraction_text.
Extract only information stated in the source text.
Do not infer missing information.
Preserve the order of appearance.
Prefer complete clauses or sentences over isolated words.
Do not extract standalone metadata, page numbers, section titles, headers, footers, table numbers, figure numbers, captions, or standalone references.
Allowed extraction classes:
- requirement: mandatory or prohibited action, usually expressed by shall, must, required, prohibited, or equivalent language.
- condition: if/when clause that controls applicability, eligibility, or triggering of another artifact.
- exception: case where a normal rule does not apply, or where approval, waiver, or special circumstance changes the rule.
- constraint: limitation, boundary, restriction, or scope limit.
- procedure: ordered steps or workflow for completing a task.
- method: analytical, design, testing, calculation, or evaluation approach.
- formula: mathematical expression or calculation rule.
- parameter: named variable, coefficient, input, output, design value, or material property.
- threshold: numeric or categorical boundary used for classification, selection, or decision-making.
- definition: explanation of the meaning of a term.
- actor_role: person, office, organization, or role responsible for action, review, approval, or consultation.
- deliverable: report, form, drawing, submission, record, package, analysis, or other output.
- assumption: design premise or condition accepted as true for analysis.
- finding: stated observation, result, diagnosis, or conclusion.
- recommendation: suggested or preferred action that is not strictly mandatory.
- best_practice: preferred, accepted, or commonly recommended practice for design, construction, testing, evaluation, documentation, or maintenance.
- decision: selected option, approval, rejection, determination, or adopted conclusion.
- rationale: explanation of why a requirement, recommendation, decision, finding, or method exists.
- issue: identified problem, deficiency, conflict, or gap.
- risk: possible adverse outcome, uncertainty, or failure mode.
- evidence: data, observation, test result, measurement, or cited basis supporting a finding, decision, rationale, or requirement.
Common attributes may include:
- subject: what the artifact is mainly about.
- scope: where, when, or to what the artifact applies.
- context_reference: table, figure, section, form, standard, or document reference mentioned inside the extracted span.
- source_cue: wording that signals the artifact type, such as shall, should, if, except, because, required, recommended.
- description: brief normalized description of the artifact, grounded in the source span.
- significance: why the artifact matters, only if the source text states or directly supports its purpose, effect, consequence, or importance.
Boundary rules:
- Use requirement when the text imposes a mandatory or prohibited action.
- Use recommendation when the text suggests a specific action but does not make it mandatory.
- Use best_practice when the text describes a generally accepted or preferred practice.
- Use method when the text names or describes an analytical, design, testing, or calculation approach.
- Use procedure when the text describes ordered steps or a workflow.
- Use condition when the text controls when something applies.
- Use exception when the text describes a case where the normal rule changes.
- Use constraint when the text limits scope, use, applicability, or allowable choices.
- Use finding when the text states an observation, result, diagnosis, or conclusion.
- Use evidence when the text states data, measurements, test results, or observations that support another artifact.
- Use rationale when the text explains why something is required, recommended, selected, observed, or used.
- Use significance only when the source text states or directly supports the artifact's purpose, effect, consequence, or importance.
Do not use these as extraction classes:
table, figure, reference, metadata, section_title, page_number, header, footer, table_of_contents_entry, caption_alone, revision_date, document_title.
If a table, figure, section, form, or external standard is mentioned inside a meaningful artifact, keep it only as an attribute such as context_reference or source_reference.
