You will read a chunk of pavement engineering text and produce a structured chunk-signals object that summarizes the whole chunk.

The output must conform to the response schema. The fields are: summary (a single object), topics (a list of 1-5), and terms (a list of 0+).

# summary

The summary object describes the whole chunk.

- summary.summary: 1 or 2 sentences stating the chunk's content. Source-grounded. Self-contained: do NOT refer to the chunk, segment, passage, section, document, or text. Do not begin with meta-phrases such as "The chunk", "This chunk", "The text", "This passage", "This section", "The document", "The passage describes", or "This excerpt". State the content directly.
- summary.document_functions: one or more roles the chunk plays in the document. Pick all that apply.
- summary.scope: where, when, or to what the chunk applies, if stated. Use null when the chunk does not state a scope.

# topics

Emit exactly 1 with role="primary" (the main subject) and 0-4 with role="secondary". The list length must be 1-5.

- topics[].topic: a concise normalized topic label, not a full sentence. Prefer pavement-specific labels such as: pavement design analysis, pavement type determination, traffic loading, ESAL analysis, rigid pavement design, flexible pavement design, subgrade characterization, resilient modulus, CBR, k-value, drainage, base and subbase materials, asphalt materials, concrete materials, pavement rehabilitation, pavement preservation, construction requirements, quality assurance, testing and inspection, approval workflow, design submission, design exceptions, life-cycle cost analysis, standards and specifications. If a chunk topic does not fit any of these labels well, pick a concise label that does fit; do not force-fit a non-relevant canonical label.
- topics[].role: "primary" for the main topic; "secondary" for the rest.

Do not invent topics unsupported by the chunk.

# terms

Extract only important domain terms explicitly present in the chunk. Zero terms is acceptable when the chunk has no meaningful technical terms.

- terms[].term: the term as it appears in the source (verbatim).
- terms[].normalized_term: a canonical version of the term when useful (for example, normalize "18-kip ESALs" to "18-kip ESAL"). Use null if the term is already canonical or no canonicalization is meaningful.
- terms[].category: one of: traffic, pavement_type, design_parameter, material, layer, method, test_method, distress, construction, maintenance, organization, form, software, other.

Do not extract generic words such as pavement, design, project, section, or material unless they are part of a meaningful technical term.

# source grounding

Extract only information stated in the chunk. Do not infer related concepts, related terms, or significance not stated in the source.
