# Scoring Schema

Assign the chunk exactly one of these three scores:

**1 = clearly relevant.** The chunk contains substantive pavement-engineering content such as:
- Concepts, methods, findings, processes, technical detail
- Observations, data, recommendations, requirements, analysis
- Definitions, specifications
- Project case studies (descriptions of specific construction projects with location, contractor, performance data)
- Conclusions or summary discussions

**0.5 = unsure / mixed / ambiguous.** Use this score when:
- The chunk fragment is too short or lacks sufficient context to judge intent
- The chunk mixes substantive content with closed-list noise categories
- The content is plausibly relevant but you are not confident

Reserve 0.5 for genuine uncertainty. Do not use it as a default.

**0 = clearly noise.** The chunk is essentially one of the closed-list noise categories defined below. Apply this only when you are confident.

# Closed-List Noise Categories (score = 0)

Label a chunk as 0 only if it is essentially one of these:

- Sponsor / funder lists
- Principal investigator / author / contact blocks (names, phones, emails, postal addresses, ORCID IDs, agreement officer info)
- Mission / vision statements (e.g. "The mission of … is to …")
- Copyright / disclaimer / liability notices
- Distribution & availability notices ("This document is available from …")
- Key Words / index-term / classification lists
- References / bibliography sections (whole sections of citation entries, not in-content citations)
- Isolated figure or table captions sitting alone with no surrounding content
- Page headers / footers / running titles / publication identifiers (e.g. "FHWA-HIF-22-020")
- Table-of-contents fragments
- Quality-assurance / endorsement / non-endorsement statements

# Special Cases

**In-content citations remain relevant:** A sentence containing "(Snyder et al. 2018)" inside a content paragraph is part of the content and should be scored 1. Only standalone reference sections with lists of citation entries score 0.

**References boundary:** The References / Bibliography / Cited Standards section starts at a heading like "# REFERENCES", "# Bibliography", "**AASHTO Standards**", "**ASTM Standards**", or similar, AND consists of contiguous citation entries thereafter (typically formatted as "Author. Year. Title. Publisher." or "- *AASHTO M 319-02: …*").

Content that appears BEFORE that heading — including project case studies, CONCLUSIONS sections, summary discussions, figure captions surrounded by content, implementation findings — is CONTENT (score 1 or 0.5), even when it sits in the last few chunks of the document. Only the contiguous citation listings that follow the heading score 0.
